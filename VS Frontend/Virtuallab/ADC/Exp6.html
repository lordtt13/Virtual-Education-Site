THEORY:

JK flip-flop: Flip-flop is used to claimant the indeterminate state of RS flip-flop. It is triggered using the clock. The clock can be active high or active low or active for negative edge of the clock or active for the positive edge of the clock. When J=0 and K=0, the circuit is inactive when J=0 and K=1 and if the flip-flop is triggered by the clock, Q=0 and =1. When J=1 and K=0 and if the flip-flop is triggered by the clock, Q=1 and =0. When J=1 and K=1 and if the flip-flop is triggered by the clock, the output Q and toggles..

D FLIP-FLOP: The D (delay) flip-flop has only one input called the D input and two outputs Q and . It can be constructed from RS flip-flop by placing an inverter between R and S and assigning the symbol, D to the S input when the clock is activated output Q will take the value of input D.

T-FLIP-FLOP: T (trigger or toggle) flip-flop has only a single data (T) input, a clock input and two output Q and The T flip-flop is obtained from a JK flip-flop by connecting its J and K inputs together. The designation T comes from the ability of the flip-flop to toggle or complement its state.

PROCEDURE:

1.Plug the chips you will be using into the breadboard. Point all the chips in the same direction with pin1 at the lower-left corner. (Pin 1 is often identified by a dot or a notch next to it on the chip package)

2. Connect +5V and GND pins of each chip to the power and ground bus strips on the breadboard.

3. Switch on VCC and apply various combinations of input according to truth table

4. Verify the values of the outputs which are tabulated in the truth table.
